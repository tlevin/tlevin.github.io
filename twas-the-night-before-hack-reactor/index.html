Twas the Night Before Hack Reactor

10/04/2015
Hack Reactor Funny

Ok, lets be a little less serious now. In the spirit of "A visit from Saint Nicholas" by Clement Clark Moore, I present 'Twas the Night Before Hack Reactor'.

Twas the night before Hack Reactor, when all through the slack.
Not a cohort was sleeping, still working on the jQuery track,
The energy bars were gathered with care,
In the hopes of not falling asleep there;
The cohort were nestled all snug with their laptops in their beds,
While visions of software engineering danced in their heads;
And me in my plaid and cap,
Had just settled in for a nap;
When out on the localhost arose such a clatter,
I sprang to the logs to see what was the matter;
Away to the terminal I flew like a flash,
Pored over the errors, typing into the bash;
The laptop display and the homebrew theme glow,
Gave a lustre of midday to the javaScript objects below,
When what to my wondering eyes did appear,
But a new direction for my career!
With a curriculum so lively and quick,
I knew in a moment that Hack Reactor was my pick.
More rapid than React did the sprints they came,
And they whistled, and shouted and invoked by name:
Now Angular! Now Express! Now MongoDB and Node!
On D3! on, Backbone! on Algorithms and Code!
To the top of the stack! code past that wall!
Now dash to the lecture! dash to the presentation! dash all!
As leaves before the wild hurricane fly,
The cohort had to be agile, and spry,
So through many lines of code they flew,
And their repos full of toys and group projects grew,
And then in a twinkling I heard typing from a devotee,
The prancing and pawing on each little key,
And as I was thinking in my head, and was turning around,
Down the lecture hall the instructor came with a bound,
He was dressed all in HR swag, from his head to his toe,
And the cohort shut their stack overflow,
A bundle of toy problems he had flung on his git,
and he wouldn’t let any in the cohort quit,
His eyes, how they twinkled! his attitude merry!
He retold that joke about the iPhone and the Blackberry,
He wrapped up a lecture on data structures with a bow,
Imparting upon us all that we would need to know
